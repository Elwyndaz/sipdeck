01 · Brand rationale

The garnished deck

Sipdeck's identity is one repeated idea: a quiet monoline frame, finished with a single garnish. The wordmark is hand-drawn-feeling monoline lowercase — same confident single-stroke weight as the ink sketches on the cards — and the dot of the i is the garnish: one drop of vermouth green, the only color the brand itself ever uses. The app icon repeats it: a recipe card tilted mid-swipe, a thin-line coupe on its face, the same green dot at the rim. Everything else — warm paper tones, generous whitespace, a serif reserved for drink names — steps back so the watercolor sketches read as the art. It says "deck of recipe cards" without a single playing-card suit, and the garnish reads just as well on a Negroni as on a seedlip spritz.

Assumptions made: accent = vermouth green (reads grown-up, works for zero-proof, and doubles as the "save" color); dark mode is included, not cut; the wordmark is a custom monoline drawing, not an outlined font — so it stays a tiny hand-editable SVG.

02 · Wordmark

Monoline, lowercase, rounded caps — the stroke weight matches the card illustrations' linework. Inherits ink color via currentColor; only the i-dot is fixed to the accent. Clear space: one x-height on all sides. Minimum width 90 px; below that, use the app icon.

Opinions, firmly held: the deck moves on transform and opacity only. Spring-back is the single place overshoot is allowed — the flip must land dead, like a real card on felt. Fly-off never fades: the card leaves the screen, it doesn't evaporate. During drag, tint the card edge with save-green or skip-red at 30% past the commit threshold so the gesture's meaning is visible before release.

07 · Usage rules
✕Never fill the wordmark or icon strokes with the accent. Ink draws; green garnishes. One green dot per composition is the rule.
✕No color behind the illustrations. Cards are always #FBF7EF — no tints, gradients, or dark-mode inversion on the card surface. The sketches were painted on that paper.
✕No playing-card symbols. No suits, no pips, no fans of 52, no dealer language. The deck is recipe cards in a kitchen drawer, not a casino table.
✕Instrument Serif is for names and titles only. Never for body text, buttons, tags, or amounts — and never bolded or condensed; it ships in one weight for a reason.
✕Skip-red is feedback, not judgment. Use it only during the leftward gesture — never for errors, warnings, or destructive actions. Skipping a card is neutral; it just goes to the back.
